Reflections on Growth

My personal insights on technology, challenges, learning, and future aspirations.

a. What technologies did you use and why?

I used Tailwind CSS for styling, Lucide Icons for icons, and HTML for structuring the content. These technologies allowed me to create a modern, responsive, and visually appealing portfolio while keeping the code clean and easy to maintain.

b. Which parts of the portfolio were easiest for you?

The easiest part was writing and adding the descriptions that reflect who I am. Sharing personal insights and experiences came naturally and made the content authentic.

c. Which parts were most challenging?

I had difficulty installing Tailwind CSS initially, but I later realized the issue was due to version incompatibility. Once I resolved that, everything worked smoothly.

d. What technologies are you most interested in learning?

I am most interested in technologies related to digital editing, graphic design, and multimedia production. This includes advanced photo and video editing tools, layout design, motion graphics, and UI/UX design. I also want to improve my skills in using creative software and explore how technology can enhance visual communication, especially for publications, campaigns, and digital media.

e. What kind of applications do you want to build in the future?

In the future, I want to build applications that integrate Artificial Intelligence to make organizational tasks easier and more efficient. One example is an AI-powered attendance system for PADC that uses facial recognition to automatically record attendance. The system can detect absences, count how many times a student is absent, and automatically compute the equivalent number of bottles required based on those absences. When the data is exported to Excel, all the information—attendance records, total absences, and required bottles—will already be organized and ready for reporting.
